## Free Alternatives to Ten Percent Happier

Ten Percent Happier was founded by ABC News anchor Dan Harris after a panic attack on live television led him to meditation. The app takes a skeptic-friendly, science-based approach that appeals to people who find typical meditation apps too spiritual. But at $99.99/year, it's one of the most expensive meditation apps available. Free alternatives offer the same core meditation techniques at no cost.

## What Ten Percent Happier Charges For

The app's subscription unlocks courses taught by well-known meditation teachers like Joseph Goldstein, Sharon Salzberg, and Jeff Warren. It also includes a "coaching" feature where you can message meditation coaches for guidance. The free tier is very limited — a small handful of introductory sessions. The focus on expert teachers and coaching is what justifies the higher price, but the actual meditation techniques taught are the same ones available for free elsewhere.
