## Guruka vs Ten Percent Happier

Ten Percent Happier started with a compelling story. Dan Harris, an ABC News anchor, had a panic attack on live television, discovered meditation, and wrote a book about it. The app grew out of that book and brands itself as meditation for skeptics — no incense, no mysticism, just practical techniques backed by evidence. It features courses taught by respected teachers like Joseph Goldstein, Sharon Salzberg, and Jeff Warren. But full access costs $99.99/year, and the free tier is extremely limited. ## What Ten Percent Happier Does Well

The app's greatest strength is credibility. Dan Harris didn't come to meditation through a spiritual awakening — he came to it because nothing else worked for his anxiety. That origin story resonates with people who find most meditation apps too soft or too mystical. The app's tone is direct, sometimes funny, and never preachy.

The teacher lineup is genuinely impressive. Joseph Goldstein is one of the most respected meditation teachers in the Western Buddhist tradition. Sharon Salzberg literally wrote the book on loving-kindness meditation. Jeff Warren brings an accessible, everyman energy to his sessions. These aren't random wellness influencers — they're people who have dedicated decades to the practice.

Ten Percent Happier also offers a coaching feature where users can text with real meditation coaches for guidance. For people who feel lost or need accountability, this is a meaningful addition that most apps don't provide.

## What Ten Percent Happier Charges For

The free tier of Ten Percent Happier is one of the most restrictive in the industry. You get a small handful of introductory sessions and little else. The full course library, the complete teacher catalog, and the coaching feature all require the premium subscription at $99.99/year. Like most apps, it uses a free trial that requires a credit card, and auto-renews if you don't cancel. The coaching feature sometimes costs extra on top of the base subscription.
