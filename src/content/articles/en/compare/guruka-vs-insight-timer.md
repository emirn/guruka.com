## Guruka vs Insight Timer

Insight Timer is the largest free meditation library in the world, with over 200,000 sessions from thousands of teachers. It's free to use with an optional premium tier at $59.99/year. The sheer volume of content is impressive — but it can also be overwhelming. Guruka takes the opposite approach: a curated, browser-based meditation tool with no account required, no app to download, and no decisions to make before you start meditating.

## What Insight Timer Does Well

Insight Timer's library is genuinely massive, and the fact that most of it is free is remarkable. If you're looking for a specific style of meditation — Vipassana, loving-kindness, body scan, yoga nidra, or something more niche — there's a good chance someone on Insight Timer teaches it. The variety is unmatched.

The community features are another strength. Live group meditations, discussion groups, and the ability to see how many people are meditating around the world create a sense of shared practice. For meditators who value community and connection, this social layer is meaningful.

The timer feature itself (the app's namesake) is excellent — a simple, customizable meditation timer with interval bells and ambient sounds. It's one of the best standalone timers available and has been a core feature since the app launched.

## What Insight Timer Charges For

Insight Timer's free tier is more generous than Headspace or Calm, but it comes with trade-offs. The free version includes ads, and offline access requires the premium subscription at $59.99/year. Premium also unlocks curated courses, a simpler interface with fewer distractions, and advanced timer features.

The bigger cost of the free tier isn't financial — it's cognitive. With 200,000+ sessions of wildly varying quality, finding a good meditation can take longer than the meditation itself. Sessions range from professionally recorded guided meditations to amateur recordings with background noise. There's no consistent quality control, and the sheer number of choices can become a barrier to actually practicing.
