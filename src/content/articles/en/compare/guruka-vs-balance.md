## Guruka vs Balance

Balance is a meditation app that uses AI-driven personalization to tailor sessions to your experience level and goals. Each day it asks check-in questions and adapts its guided meditations based on your answers. The app covers focus, sleep, stress reduction, and other categories, and it's genuinely well-designed. Balance gained popularity by offering a free first year — but once that year ends, the subscription kicks in at $69.99/year. Many users discover they've built a meditation habit that now costs money to maintain. [Guruka](/) offers guided meditation that's free forever, with no account required and no expiration date.

## What Balance Does Well

Balance's personalization is not a gimmick. The app genuinely adapts to how you're feeling each day. The check-in questions are quick, and the resulting sessions feel more relevant than picking randomly from a library. Over time, the app learns your preferences and adjusts difficulty, duration, and focus areas. For beginners especially, this removes the overwhelming "what should I do today?" problem.

The app design itself is excellent. Balance is one of the most polished meditation apps on the market — clean interface, smooth animations, and thoughtful touches throughout. The audio quality is high, and the session pacing feels natural rather than rushed or artificially slow.

The free first year is genuinely generous compared to most competitors that offer 7-day trials. A full year gives you enough time to build a real meditation practice and explore everything the app offers. The problem isn't the free year — it's what happens after.

## What Balance Charges For

After the free first year, Balance costs $69.99/year. There's no permanently free tier — once the trial ends, you either pay or lose access to everything, including the personalized sessions you've grown accustomed to. This creates a lock-in effect: you've spent a year building a meditation habit inside Balance, and now leaving means starting over somewhere else. Some users feel this was the strategy all along — get people dependent on the personalization, then charge them to keep it.
