## Guruka vs Headspace

Headspace is one of the most recognized names in meditation. Founded by Andy Puddicombe, a former Buddhist monk, it offers structured courses, animations, and a polished onboarding experience. It costs $69.99/year after a free trial that requires a credit card. Guruka is a free, browser-based meditation tool that requires no account, no download, and no payment — you open the page and start meditating.

## What Headspace Does Well

Headspace's greatest strength is its structured approach to teaching meditation. The Basics course walks beginners through 30 days of foundational practice, building skills progressively. Andy Puddicombe's voice and teaching style are genuinely calming, and the short animated videos that explain meditation concepts are some of the best educational content in the space.

The app's design is clean and friendly. It doesn't feel clinical or overly spiritual — it feels approachable. For people who have never meditated before, Headspace does an excellent job of making the practice feel accessible and non-intimidating.

Headspace also offers themed packs for specific goals like stress, focus, and sleep, each structured as multi-session courses. If you're someone who thrives on structured programs and progressive learning, Headspace delivers that well.

## What Headspace Charges For

Headspace locks roughly 95% of its content behind its $69.99/year subscription. The free tier includes about 10 introductory sessions and a few rotating daily meditations. Sleep content, focus music, full courses, and move exercises are all premium-only.

The free trial requires a credit card upfront and auto-renews if you don't cancel. This is a common source of frustration — Headspace has a Trustpilot score of approximately 1.8 out of 5, with many complaints about unexpected charges and difficult cancellation.
