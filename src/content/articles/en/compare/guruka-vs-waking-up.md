## Guruka vs Waking Up

Waking Up is Sam Harris's meditation app, built around a secular, philosophical approach to mindfulness and non-dual awareness. It costs $99.99/year, though a free subscription is available if you email the team and request one. The app offers a 28-day introductory course, theory lessons, and conversations with meditation teachers. Guruka is a free, browser-based meditation tool — no account, no download, no payment, no need to request access.

## What Waking Up Does Well

Waking Up is arguably the most intellectually serious meditation app available. Sam Harris brings a neuroscience background and a philosophical rigor that appeals to people who want to understand why they're meditating, not just how. The Theory section includes lessons on consciousness, free will, and the nature of the self — topics you won't find on any other meditation app.

The 28-day introductory course is excellent. It builds progressively, introducing concepts like focused attention, open awareness, and eventually non-dual awareness in a way that feels like genuine teaching rather than a scripted relaxation exercise. Harris is a skilled communicator, and his daily meditations are concise and substantive.

The app also features conversations with other teachers and thinkers — Joseph Goldstein, Loch Kelly, Diana Winston, and others — giving users exposure to different contemplative traditions. For meditators who want depth and variety in their teachers, this is a real strength.

## What Waking Up Charges For

Waking Up costs $99.99/year, making it one of the more expensive meditation apps. To their credit, the team offers free subscriptions to anyone who emails and says they can't afford it — no questions asked. This is a genuinely generous policy, but it still requires you to ask, create an account, and download the app.

The free trial gives you access to the first few sessions of the introductory course. Everything else — the full course, daily meditations, theory lessons, conversations, and the meditation timer — requires a subscription or an approved free access request.
